## The Problem with Post-LOI Diligence

In veterinary M&A, critical market intelligence often surfaces after Letters of Intent are submitted. By that point, deal momentum makes it difficult to walk away, even when red flags emerge.

## What Gets Missed Before LOI

### Talent Availability

Most investors assess staffing feasibility after the deal is signed. The questions that should be asked pre-LOI:

- How many licensed DVMs exist within realistic hiring distance?
- What is the competitive density for veterinary talent?
- Are there specialty credentialing requirements that limit the candidate pool?
- What is the historical turnover rate in this market?

### Competitive Saturation

Ownership consolidation is accelerating, but visibility into who owns what remains opaque. Shell LLCs and holding entities obscure true competitive dynamics.

Smart investors validate:

- Actual practice ownership structures in the target market
- Recent acquisitions and expansion activity
- Pricing pressure from corporate consolidation
- Market share concentration risks

### Market Dynamics

Geographic expansion looks attractive on paper, but reality surfaces in the data:

- Drive-time accessibility for clients and staff
- Demographic trends affecting pet ownership
- Practice density and revenue concentration
- Reputation signals from review platforms

## The Hidden Cost of Incomplete Inputs

When diligence happens too late, investors face:

- **Post-close staffing crises**: Discovering the talent pool is smaller than assumed
- **Unexpected competitive pressure**: Realizing consolidation has already saturated the market
- **Integration challenges**: Finding ownership structures don't match representations
- **Portfolio risk compounding**: Issues replicating across multiple geographies
