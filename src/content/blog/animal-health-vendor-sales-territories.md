## The Problem with Traditional Territory Design

Most animal health vendors design sales territories using geography as the primary variable: assign zip codes, balance headcount, and hope for the best. The problem? Geography doesn't predict revenue.

## Why Zip Code Territories Fail

Traditional territory design assumes uniform practice distribution and opportunity. Reality looks different:

- **Practice concentration varies dramatically**: Urban markets have dense clusters; rural areas have sparse coverage
- **Ownership changes break account structures**: Corporate consolidation creates multi-location decision-making that zip codes don't capture
- **Practice capability isn't visible**: Not all clinics can operationalize every product
- **Reps waste time on dead accounts**: Closed, relocated, or misclassified practices consume sales capacity

## The Hidden Costs

When territories are built on broken inputs, vendors experience:

- Reps spending time on unreachable or irrelevant accounts
- Unequal territory potential leading to quota gaming
- Missed opportunities in underserved, high-density areas
- CRM data degradation as bad inputs compound
